## Why Outside-In

"How do you sell?" puts founders on the defensive.  
"How are your buyers coming to a yes?" surfaces the same information from the buyer's experience.

The answers reveal which decisions are locked, which are fuzzy, and which are missing â€” without the founder feeling audited.

Each zone has a name that describes the property the venture either has or lacks â€” assessed from the outside:

- A buyer assesses **Buyability**
- The market assesses **Viability**
- A committee member assesses **Travelability**
- A target prospect assesses **Reachability**
- A prospect at decision assesses **Closability**
- An existing customer assesses **Stickability** and **Expandability**
- An investor assesses **Fundability**
- A new market assesses **Portability**
- Talent assesses **Thriveability**

---

## On Desirability and Feasibility

The classic design triangle asks: desirable, feasible, viable?  
These are preconditions. This framework starts where the triangle ends â€” assuming genuine demand exists and something has been built.

**Desirability** is implied in Zone 1 (Buyability). If Zone 1 signals are consistently fuzzy or missing, dig deeper into whether demand is real before locking positioning decisions.

**Feasibility** is embedded in Zone 2 (Viability) as delivery capacity. In most software ventures today, technical build is no longer the constraint â€” delivery and scaling are.

## Zone 6: Stickability

**Activate when:** 10+ customers exist AND retention/activation signals are weak.

**Outside-in question:**

> **"What brings a customer back after first use? What's the moment they stop treating it as a trial and start depending on it?"**

### Decisions underneath:

- Activation moment â€” the one event that means "they got it"

- Habit loop â€” what pulls them back weekly/monthly?

- Churn triggers â€” early warning signals + intervention plan

### Surfaces:

- Second-use rate

### Lagging indicators:

- Gross revenue retention (GRR)
